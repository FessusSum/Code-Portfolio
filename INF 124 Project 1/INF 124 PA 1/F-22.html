F-22 "Raptor"

Name: F-22 "Raptor"
Role: Stealth Air Superiority Fighter
Manufacturer: Lockheed Martin
National Origin: United States
Unit Cost: $150,000,000

General Characteristics
Crew: Pilot
Length: 18.92 meters
Wingspan: 13.56 meters
Maximum Speed: Mach 1.95


The F-22 "Raptor" is the first and currently only fifth generation fighter that is combat ready. Featuring a previously unheard of combination of supercruise capability, supermaneuverability, stealth, and sensor fusion, the F-22 is a force to be reckoned with. Thanks to its powerful engines, the F-22 can supercruise, flying at supersonic speeds without wasting fuel on afterburners and allowing it to readily intercept aircraft before they can reach their targets. On approach, the F-22 is nearly impossible to detect by radar thanks to its reduced radar cross section and radar-absorbent airframe. Unlike other stealth aircraft that rely on these techniques, the F-22 maintains its level of stealth with far less maintenence required.

The F-22 was designed with stealth in mind. As such, it is very difficult to detect by radar or by the naked eye, thanks to several design measures such as the use of radar-absorbent material, fixed-gemoetry serpentine inlets, flat thrust-vectoring nozzles, reduced emissions, and acoustic signatures. Furthermore, the internal weapons bays and cannon make sure that the F-22 only reveals itself when it fires upon an unsuspecting target when it is too late to react. Unlike other stealth aircraft, the F-22 does not require special maintenance processes and can be stored and repaired in a normal hangar.

Armament: 20mm M61A2 Vulcan cannon, 3 internal weapons bays capable of holding 8 missiles in total, 4 external hardpoints with total capacity of 9080 kg
