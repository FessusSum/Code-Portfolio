F-2A

Name: F-2A
Role: Multirole Fighter
Manufacturer: Mitsubishi Heavy Industries
National Origin: Japan/United States
Unit Cost: $127,000,000

General Characteristics
Crew: Pilot
Length: 15.52 meters
Wingspan: 11.13 meters
Maximum Speed: Mach 2.0


Based on the F-16 platform, the F-2A is the Japanese Air Self Defense Force's primary multirole fighter. While externally similar to the F-16, the J-2A features drastically overhauled electronics in comparison. The most notable upgrade is the inclusion of an active electronically scanned array, or ASEA, which is much more difficult to detect and jam. Other improvements include modern composite materials and a larger wing area to make space for more munitions.

While visually similar, the F-2A boasts a number of important improvements over the F-16. The wings are 25% larger to accommodate a wider selection of missiles and other ordinance. Similarly, the nose has been elongated and widened to make room for the AESA radar. Finally, there are a number of smaller improvements to the airframe such as a larger tailplane and air intake.

Armament: 20mm JM61A1 cannon, 11 hardpoints with total capacity of 8085 kg
