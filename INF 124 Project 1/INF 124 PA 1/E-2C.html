The E-2 Hawkeye is a carrier-based Airborn Warning and Control Systems aircraft. Equipped with twin turboprop engines, retractrable tricycle landing gear, and a tail hook, the E-2 is a versatile all-weather support aircraft capable of lending support to any carrier battle group. Its primary function is as an AWACS unit, performing command and control of the battlespace during aerial engagements by directing fighter and attack aircraft strikes. Many navies, including the United States Navy, use AWACS units to augment and protect carrier Command Information Centers, giving friendly units an often decisive advantage in battle. Modern AWACS aircraft like the E-2 generally operate 400km away from the Area of Operations, well out of the way of most anti-aircraft missiles and other enemy counterattacks. As a testament to the E-2's extremely long radar range, three E-2s in overlapping orbit can cover all of Central Europe. While many AWACS units are land-based, the E-2 can be launched from carriers, greatly extending its normal operational range.

The E-2C features upgraded propeller systems for better maintainability through all conditions. In addition, the E-2C also is equipped with an improved mission computer and upgraded Combat Information Center workstations compared to the earlier E-2A and E-2B. This allows for faster processing and access to satelite information networks thanks to an onboard data-link system. Unfortunately, the APS-145 radar currently equipped on the E-2C is not capable of detecting advanced fifth-generation stealth fighters such as the Russian T-50 or Chinese J-20. To counter this, the E-2D Advanced Hawkeye, featuring an entirely new avoinics suite including the superior AN/APY 9 radar, is slated to replace the aging E-2Cs currently in active service with the United States Navy in the coming years. E-2Cs in other nations are also being phased out in favor of more modern AWACS units.
