Role: Carrier-based Multirole Fighter
Manufacturer: McDonnell Douglas/Boeing
National Origin: United States
Unit Cost: $98,300,000

General Characteristics
Crew: Pilot
Length: 18.31 meters
Wingspan: 13.62 meters
Maximum Speed: Mach 1.55

The F/A-18E "Super Hornet" is the carrier based derivative of the older F/A-18 "Hornet". Replacing the F-14, the F/A-18E shares many characteristics and equipment with the F/A-18 for easier integration into combined service. Despite this, there are numerous improvements made on the design of the F/A-18 over its predecessor including a larger fuel capacity, heavier maximum weight, and the ability for in-flight refueling. These characteristics make the F/A-18E the perfect choice for complementing a carrier strike group, extending the fleet's reach by a significant margin.

The F/A-18E was made with survivability in mind rather than stealth. Rather than relying on stealth, the aircraft uses advanced electronic warfare capabilities and superior avionics software to outperform the enemy. The most powerful characteristic of the "Super Hornet", however, is its "growth space" for its avionics. This allows for seamless updates to the aircraft software as newer technology becomes available, allowing the F/A-18E to remain competitive against newer aircraft. Whatever the scenario, there is no battlefield or enemy the F/A-18E cannot handle.

Armament: 20mm M61A2 Vulcan cannon, 11 hardpoints with total capacity of 8050 kg
