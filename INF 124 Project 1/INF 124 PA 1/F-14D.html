F-14D "Super Tomcat"

Name: F-14D "Super Tomcat"
Role: Interceptor, Multirole Combat Aircraft
Manufacturer: Northropp Grumman
National Origin: United States
Unit Cost: $38,000,000

General Characteristics
Crew: Pilot, Radar Intercept Officer
Length: 19.55 meters
Wingspan: 19.55 meters
Maximum Speed: Mach 2.01


The final variant of the F-14, the F-14D "Super Tomcat" is heavily upgraded compared to its predecessors. Equipped with newer GE F110-400 engines and upgraded digital avionics systems, the F-14D features vastly increased performance over its contemporary peers. The signature variable-sweep wings on the F-14D allow the aircraft to control the lift-to-drag ratio and consequently, its speed. This allows the F-14D to quickly move to intercept incoming hostile aircraft and then slow down to optimal speeds for engagement.

The F-14D was meant to act as an all-purpose carrier-based strike fighter, being able to launch from anywhere in the world to perform any kind of mission. Its variable-sweep wings allowed it to act both as a long range interceptor and an air superiority fighter. Inside the aircraft's bulky nose contains the hefty amount of electronics the pilot needs to fly, including a Central Air Data Computer that maintains the optimal angle for the wings during flight.

Armament: 20mm M61A1 Vulcan cannon, 10 hardpoints with total capacity of 6,600 kg
