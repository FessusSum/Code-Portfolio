General Characteristics

Maximum Speed: Mach 2


Originally designed for air superiority, the F-16 "Fighting Falcon" eventually evolved into a all-weather multirole fighter. While "Fighting Falcon" may be its official name, pilots and crew have taken to referring to the aircraft as the "Viper" for its resemblance to the animal. Every bit as deadly as its namesake, the F-16C embodies versatility with its multiple weapon hardpoints and robust systems. This power comes at a relatively cheap cost, being inexpensive to manufacture and cheaper to maintain.

Owing to its air superiority roots, the F-16 was designed to be slightly aerodynamically unstable as opposed to most other fighter aircraft. This gave the aircraft increased maneuverability at the cost of controlled flight. To counter the aircraft's unpredictability, the F-16 is equipped with a quadruplex fly-by-wire flight control system that automatically adjusts the craft to stay on the pilot-set flight path.

Armament: 20mm M61A1 Vulcan cannon, 11 hardpoints with total capacity of 7700 kg
