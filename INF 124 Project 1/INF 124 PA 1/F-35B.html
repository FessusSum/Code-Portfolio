Name: F-35B "Lightning II"

Manufacturer: Lockheed Martin
National Origin: United States

General Characteristics
Crew: Pilot
Length: 15.67 meters
Wingspan: 10.7 meters
Maximum Speed: Mach 1.6


The F-35B is the Short Take Off and Vertical Landing, or STOVL, variant of the F-35 and is capable of taking off from a much shorter distance of 15 meters. This allows the F-35B to take off from damaged runways or even no runway at all. While other aircraft need a full length runway to take off, the F-35B can be in the air from a near stationary position. Furthermore, the F-35B is capable of a completely vertical landing, behaving more like a helicopter than a jet fighter. This trait combined with the extremely short take-off length means that the F-35 can deploy from nearly any location in the world regardless of conditions.

Mostly similar to the F-35A, the main difference between the F-35B and F-35A is the presence of the vertical flight system in the center of the craft.
