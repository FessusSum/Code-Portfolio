A-10 "Thunderbolt II"

Name: A-10 "Thunderbolt II"
Role: Close Air Support and Ground Attack
Manufacturer: Fairchild Republic
National Origin: United States
Unit Cost: $18,800,000

General Characteristics
Crew: Pilot
Length: 16.26 meters
Wingspan: 17.53 meters
Maximum Speed: Mach 0.57


The A-10 "Thunderbolt II" is a twin turbofan engine, straight wing attack aircraft designed for sustained close air support of ground forces. Envisioned as a direct improvement on the aging and inadequate A-1 "Skyraider"", the A-10 is designed around the massive 30mm GAU-8 Avenger rotary autocannon which can disable modern tanks by saturating them with alternating 30mm depleted high explosive uranium rounds and armor piercing incendiary rounds. In addition, the A-10 comes with 11 hardpoints for a total of 7,260kg of munitions. The A-10's airframe was designed with durability in mind, made with a simple design requiring minimal maintenance and 540kg of titanium armor. While close air support is largely performed by attack helicopters such as the AH-1Z "Viper" or Mi-28 "Havoc" in most modern militaries, the A-10 is called upon for situations deemed too dangerous for helicopter units.

The A-10 has received many upgrades to the onboard avionics including an updated IFF system and Precision Engagement upgrades. The modern A-10 is capable of being deployed from the most primitive of forward airfields allowing it to provide support to ground forces even at the farthest reaches of the battlefield. Able to be refueled, rearmed, and repaired with minimal equipment, the A-10 is capable of operating in nearly every scenario. The A-10's most defining characteristic is its durability, being capable of suffering damage that would otherwise ground more fragile airframes.

Armament: 30mm GAU-8/A Avenger rotary autocannon, 11 munitions hardpoints with a combined capacity of 7,260 kg.
